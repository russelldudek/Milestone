Days 1-30

Read the program before redesigning it.

Outcome

A shared, evidence-based view of the four-workstream portfolio, decision rights, budget, roadmap, dependencies, risk, quality/compliance boundaries, and change burden.

Actions

Stakeholder and authority map

  • Confirm sponsors, steering members, R&D scientific owners, data scientists, ML engineers, Digital/IT, architecture, security, privacy, quality, compliance, finance, change partners, procurement, and vendors.
  • Document decision rights, escalation paths, forum purpose, and required pre-reads.

Portfolio truth

  • Reconcile roadmap, backlog, scope, milestones, budget baseline, actuals, forecast, resource plan, risks, issues, assumptions, dependencies, and value hypotheses.
  • Identify missing or conflicting sources of truth.

Delivery-system observation

  • Attend existing agile ceremonies and governance forums before changing them.
  • Map handoffs, queueing, blocked work, rework, and time lost waiting for decisions.

R&D and control-function empathy

  • Understand what scientists need protected: methodological rigor, reproducibility, interpretation, traceability, and expert judgment.
  • Understand quality, compliance, data-governance, privacy, and security obligations at the point where they affect delivery.

Days 31-60

Install the common protocol.

Outcome

A lightweight operating cadence that gives every workstream a common decision language while retaining fit-for-purpose scientific and technical delivery.

Mechanisms

MechanismWhat changesWhy it matters
AI Portfolio ProtocolEach workstream maintains value, readiness, authority, spend, dependencies, and adoption ownershipSteering decisions compare like-for-like evidence without flattening methods
Decision-led steeringPre-reads lead with exceptions, tradeoffs, forecast movement, and decisions requiredExecutive time produces commitments rather than status narration
Integrated roadmapMilestones show cross-workstream and enterprise dependencies, not only team datesCritical-path risk appears early
Budget confidence viewActuals, forecast, assumptions, ranges, and trigger points are visibleUncertainty becomes governable rather than hidden
Change evidenceUsers, workflow changes, enablement, feedback, and sustainment owners join delivery evidenceAdoption is engineered before launch
Risk/issue disciplineRisks are linked to owners, triggers, mitigations, decisions, and due datesRisk registers drive action rather than archival reporting

Agile coaching stance

Use Scrum, Kanban, SAFe, or hybrid practices as means, not identity. Preserve what helps teams learn and deliver; remove ceremonies that do not change priorities, unblock work, improve quality, or create learning. Measure flow and decision quality before declaring “maturity.”

Deliverables by day 60

  • Accepted portfolio protocol and common status definitions.
  • Revised steering cadence and concise executive readout.
  • Integrated dependency and decision log.
  • Budget/forecast narrative with assumptions and escalation triggers.
  • Change/adoption evidence embedded in each workstream.

Days 61-90

Prove the cadence through real decisions.

Outcome

The portfolio operating system has survived live workstream decisions, exposed its weak points, and begun transferring into durable client capability.

Actions

Run decision rehearsals

Use upcoming sponsor decisions to test evidence quality, authority clarity, budget confidence, dependency readiness, and adoption ownership before the formal forum.

Retire low-value reporting

Remove duplicate status fields and meetings that do not affect a decision, risk, commitment, or learning loop.

Transfer ownership

Pair each mechanism with a named owner, operating instructions, examples, and a feedback cadence so it does not depend on the contract role.

Executive scorecard categories

CategoryQuestionEvidence type
ValueIs the outcome hypothesis clearer and more supportable?Verified baseline, user evidence, business/scientific measure
FlowAre work and decisions moving with less avoidable waiting?Blocked time, dependency aging, decision latency
ConfidenceDoes leadership understand forecast and uncertainty?Actuals, range, assumptions, variance narrative

Day-90 review

Present what has changed, which decisions improved, which mechanisms earned continued use, what should stop, what remains uncertain, and the next-quarter priorities. No invented maturity score; the evidence itself is the report.
