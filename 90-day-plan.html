Days 1-60

Read the program, then install the common protocol.

Outcome by day 60

A shared evidence-based view of the four-workstream portfolio, explicit decision rights, a reconciled roadmap and budget narrative, visible dependencies, and a lightweight governance cadence that teams can use without slowing legitimate scientific and technical work.

Days 1-30 · Read the program

Stakeholder and authority map

  • Confirm sponsors, steering members, R&D scientific owners, data scientists, ML engineers, Digital/IT, architecture, security, privacy, quality/compliance, finance, change partners, procurement, and vendors.
  • Document decision rights, escalation paths, forum purposes, and required pre-reads.

Portfolio truth

  • Reconcile roadmap, backlog, milestones, budget baseline, actuals, forecast, resource plan, risks, issues, assumptions, dependencies, and value hypotheses.
  • Separate missing evidence from conflicting versions of truth.

Delivery-system observation

  • Attend ceremonies and governance forums before changing them.
  • Map handoffs, queueing, blocked work, work-in-progress, rework, and time lost waiting for decisions.

Agile coaching stance

Use Scrum, Kanban, SAFe, or hybrid practices as means, not identity. Preserve what helps teams learn and deliver; remove ceremonies that do not change priorities, unblock work, improve quality, or create learning. Measure flow and decision quality before declaring maturity.

Deliverables by day 60

  • Accepted protocol vocabulary and common status definitions
  • Revised steering cadence and concise executive readout
  • Integrated dependency and decision log
  • Budget/forecast narrative with assumptions and escalation triggers
  • Change/adoption evidence embedded in each workstream

Days 61-90

Prove the cadence through real decisions and transfer ownership.

Outcome by day 90

The portfolio operating system has survived real workstream decisions, exposed its weak points, improved sponsor confidence, and begun transferring into durable client capability for the remaining engagement horizon.

Actions

Run decision rehearsals

Use upcoming sponsor decisions to test evidence quality, authority clarity, budget confidence, dependency readiness, adoption ownership, and the usefulness of the forum.

Retire low-value reporting

Remove duplicate status fields and meetings that do not affect a decision, risk, commitment, forecast, or learning loop.

Transfer ownership

Pair each mechanism with a named owner, operating instructions, examples, acceptance criteria, and a feedback cadence.

Executive scorecard categories

CategoryQuestionEvidence type
ValueIs the outcome hypothesis clearer and more supportable?Verified baseline, user evidence, business/scientific measure
FlowAre work and decisions moving with less avoidable waiting?Blocked time, dependency aging, decision latency
ConfidenceDoes leadership understand forecast and uncertainty?Actuals, range, assumptions, variance narrative
TrustAre authority, quality, privacy, and compliance controls explicit?Decision rights, evaluations, exceptions, signoffs
AdoptionIs the capability becoming part of real R&D work?Usage, workflow integration, feedback, sustainment owner
LearningDoes one workstream improve the next?Reusable patterns, rejected assumptions, changed standards

Transfer package

  • Portfolio protocol guide and definitions
  • Steering pre-read and decision-record templates
  • Roadmap, dependency, budget, risk, and change conventions
  • Facilitation notes and coaching prompts
  • Examples of advance, re-plan, escalate, hold, and stop decisions
  • Owner map and operating calendar

Durable capability test

  • Teams can explain which evidence must mature next
  • Sponsors can distinguish status from a decision request
  • Budget changes have assumptions, ranges, and action triggers
  • Dependencies have owners and explicit consequences
  • Adoption ownership exists before launch
  • Lessons from one workstream alter the next

Day-90 review

Present what changed, which decisions improved, which mechanisms earned continued use, what should stop, what remains uncertain, and the next-quarter priorities. No invented maturity score; the evidence itself is the report.
