Workstream 1 of 4

The job beneath the title.

This is not merely sprint administration. The mandate is to install a trustworthy portfolio operating system for approximately four AI workstreams under executive, financial, scientific, technical, and compliance scrutiny - then leave it stronger than it was found.

01

Agile cadence vs. evidence cadence

Sprints move work. R&D decisions require evidence that may mature unevenly.

Separate delivery rhythm from decision readiness.
02

Portfolio consistency vs. scientific difference

Common governance cannot flatten legitimate differences among use cases, data, models, and users.

Standardize the decision fields, not the science.
03

Executive visibility vs. report theater

More status can still produce less clarity when activity is mistaken for value or risk resolution.

Report decisions, evidence, and exceptions.
04

Budget control vs. discovery uncertainty

AI work needs forecast discipline without pretending uncertain work is deterministic.

Forecast ranges, triggers, and stop conditions.
05

Governance vs. velocity

Compliance added late creates rework; governance added intelligently creates a faster path to a credible decision.

Put authority and evidence inside the work.
06

Nine-month delivery vs. durable capability

A contract program manager must deliver now while transferring mechanisms that keep working afterward.

Build the operating system and handoff together.

First 90 days: install clarity without slowing the science.

The plan starts with current-state truth, establishes decision rights and portfolio cadence, then proves the mechanisms through real workstreams and transfers ownership.

DAYS 1-30

Read the program

  • Map sponsors, workstream owners, scientific users, Digital/IT, data/ML teams, quality, compliance, finance, change partners, and vendors.
  • Reconcile roadmap, budget, delivery artifacts, dependencies, risks, and value hypotheses.
  • Define decision forums and distinguish delivery status from decision readiness.
DAYS 31-60

Install the protocol

  • Standardize the six decision fields while preserving legitimate methodological differences.
  • Launch concise steering reviews, budget/forecast signals, dependency ownership, and exception escalation.
  • Embed change and adoption evidence into sprint and review cadences.
DAYS 61-90

Prove and transfer

  • Use live workstream decisions to test the governance model and remove low-value reporting.
  • Show value, risk, spend, and roadmap tradeoffs in one executive narrative.
  • Codify cadence, artifacts, ownership, and learning for the remaining engagement.
