Candidate thesis

The portfolio is a protocol, not a pile of projects.

Real mandate

Install a trusted portfolio operating system for approximately four AI workstreams so senior leaders can make timely decisions about value, risk, spend, dependencies, quality, and adoption while technical and scientific teams retain room for legitimate discovery.

Central operating contradictions

TensionWhat it protectsWhat it can costProgram mechanism
Agile cadence vs. evidence cadenceMomentum and learningFalse certainty or delayed decisionsSeparate sprint progress from decision readiness
Portfolio consistency vs. scientific differenceComparability and controlMethodological flatteningCommon evidence fields, differentiated delivery paths
Governance vs. velocityQuality, privacy, compliance, trustLate-stage rework when controls arrive lateEmbed authority and evidence at the point of work
Budget discipline vs. discovery uncertaintyStewardship and prioritizationForecast theaterRanges, triggers, assumptions, and explicit sponsor decisions
Executive visibility vs. report theaterAlignment and accountabilityActivity without decisionsException-led steering narrative

AI Portfolio Protocol

Decision fieldQuestion the portfolio must answer
1. Value evidenceWhat outcome is expected, who owns the baseline, and what evidence supports the commitment?
2. Data/model readinessWhat data quality, access, evaluation, monitoring, and infrastructure conditions must be true?
3. Human/quality authorityWho may rely on the output, override it, approve it, investigate exceptions, and accept residual risk?
4. Spend and forecastWhat has been spent, what is forecast, what uncertainty remains, and what decision changes the commitment?
5. DependenciesWhich teams, vendors, systems, data sources, controls, or environments are on the critical path?
6. Adoption ownershipWho changes the workflow, enables users, monitors sustained use, and owns the capability after delivery?

Governance cadence

Workstream cadence

Backlog, sprint/flow, demos, technical risk, dependency actions, user feedback.

Portfolio cadence

Roadmap, budget, capacity, interdependencies, risk, change burden, value evidence.

Executive cadence

Decision required, tradeoff, forecast movement, confidence, next commitment.
