Candidate thesis

The portfolio is a protocol, not a pile of projects.

Real mandate

Install a trusted portfolio operating system for approximately four AI workstreams so senior leaders can make timely decisions about value, risk, spend, dependencies, quality, and adoption - while technical and scientific teams retain room for legitimate discovery.

Central operating contradictions

TensionWhat it protectsWhat it can costProgram mechanism
Agile cadence vs. evidence cadenceMomentum and learningFalse certainty or delayed decisionsSeparate sprint progress from decision readiness
Portfolio consistency vs. scientific differenceComparability and controlMethodological flatteningCommon evidence fields, differentiated delivery paths
Governance vs. velocityQuality, privacy, compliance, trustLate-stage rework when controls arrive lateEmbed authority and evidence at the point of work

Operating thesis

Standardize the evidence required for a governance decision - value, readiness, authority, spend, dependencies, and adoption ownership - without standardizing away the science, model behavior, workflow, or legitimate uncertainty of each workstream.

Decision architecture

AI Portfolio Protocol

1. Value evidence
What business, scientific, operational, or decision outcome is expected? Who owns the baseline and benefit realization?
2. Data/model readiness
What data quality, access, lineage, model performance, evaluation, monitoring, and infrastructure conditions must be true?
3. Human/quality authority
Who may rely on the output, override it, approve it, investigate exceptions, and accept residual risk?
4. Spend and forecast
What has been spent, what is forecast, what uncertainty remains, and what trigger changes the commitment?
5. Dependencies
Which decisions, vendors, systems, teams, data sources, controls, or environments are on the critical path?
6. Adoption ownership
Who changes the workflow, enables users, monitors sustained use, and owns the capability after delivery?

Governance cadence

Workstream cadence

Backlog, sprint/flow, demos, technical risk, dependency actions, user feedback.

Portfolio cadence

Roadmap, budget, capacity, interdependencies, risk, change burden, value evidence.

Executive cadence

Exceptions, decisions required, tradeoffs, forecast movement, value, confidence, next commitment.

Success measures to establish with the client

  • Decision latency and number of unresolved governance decisions.
  • Roadmap predictability, dependency aging, budget variance quality, and re-plan frequency.
  • Evidence completeness at review, risk retirement, and quality/compliance rework.
  • Workflow adoption, sustained use, human override/exception patterns, and operating ownership.
  • Value realization categories appropriate to each workstream, with verified baselines before targets.

Executive discovery

Questions that reveal the operating system.

1. What decision has the AI Transformation Office found hardest to make consistently across workstreams?
2. Where does the current governance model create confidence, and where does it create reporting burden without improving a decision?
3. How are R&D scientists expected to influence prioritization, success measures, and human-authority boundaries?
4. Which portfolio decisions belong to the AITO, sponsors, R&D functions, Digital/IT, quality/compliance, and finance respectively?
5. How is value currently defined for research-facing AI work where the first benefit may be better evidence or faster learning rather than immediate cost reduction?
6. What is the most consequential cross-workstream dependency today?
7. How mature are budget baselines and forecast ranges for the four workstreams?
8. Which change-management responsibilities sit inside the program versus in a separate change function?
9. What must be durable at the end of the nine-month engagement for the program to be considered a success?
10. What would make you say after 30 days that this Program Manager is increasing confidence rather than simply increasing reporting?

Evidence I would bring into the conversation

  • Current AI-first operating transformation across production, quality, support, engineering workflow, enterprise systems, telemetry, and a fielded robotics fleet.
  • Direct AI-readiness, workflow, governance, human-authority, evaluation, and adoption framework design.
  • Scaled operational cadence and launch mechanisms at Amazon.
  • R&D program, engineering, manufacturing, quality-system, and high-reliability delivery experience at Compunetics.
  • Scientific grounding and regulated operating judgment without claiming undisclosed pharma tenure.

Interview close

I would ask to work through one anonymized workstream decision together: what leadership needs to decide, what evidence exists, which authority boundaries matter, where the budget or dependency uncertainty sits, and who must own adoption. The quality of that conversation is more predictive than a generic claim of transformation experience.
